Grammar RulesGRAMMAR
  • Oxford Comma: Use a comma before the last item in a series of 3+
  • Its vs. It's: "its" = possessive. "it's" = it is
  • There/Their/They're: location / possession / they are
  • Semicolon: Joins two independent clauses without a conjunction
  • Dangling modifier: Modifier must be next to what it modifies
  • Active vs Passive: "The dog bit the man" vs "The man was bitten"

Scientific MethodMETHOD
  • Independent Variable: What you change. X-axis. The cause.
  • Dependent Variable: What you measure. Y-axis. The effect.
  • Control Group: Baseline — receives no treatment.
  • Hypothesis format: If [IV] then [DV] because [reasoning].
  • Order: Observation → Question → Hypothesis → Experiment → Data → Conclusion
Stoichiometry RoadmapCHEM
  • Route: grams A → moles A → moles B → grams B
  • Moles: moles = mass(g) ÷ molar mass(g/mol)
  • Mole ratio: from coefficients in the balanced equation
  • Limiting reagent: substance that runs out first — controls yield
  • % yield: (actual ÷ theoretical) × 100

Periodic TrendsPERIODIC
  • Atomic radius: Increases down a group, decreases across a period (left to right)
  • Electronegativity: Increases up and to the right. Fluorine is highest.
  • Ionization energy: Increases up and to the right.
  • Metal character: Increases down and to the left.

GeneticsGENETICS
  • Dominant vs Recessive: Dominant (A) masks recessive (a). aa = recessive phenotype.
  • Punnett square: Cross two genotypes to predict offspring ratios.

  • Hardy-Weinberg: p²+2pq+q² = 1 | p+q = 1

  • Codons: 3 nucleotide sequence codes for 1 amino acid. 64 possible codons.

  • Product Ruled/dx[uv] = u'v + uv'
  • Quotient Ruled/dx[u/v] = (u'v − uv') / v²
  • d/dx sin, cosd/dx[sin x]=cos x | d/dx[cos x]=−sin x

LimitsLIMITS

  • Continuity: f is continuous at a if lim(x→a) f(x) = f(a)
